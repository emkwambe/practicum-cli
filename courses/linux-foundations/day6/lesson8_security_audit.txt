# Lesson: Security Audit — Who Has Access to What?

## [WHEN] You need this when:
1. After a security incident, review who accessed the system.
2. A compliance audit requires listing all users and their access.
3. You need to find files with dangerous permissions (777).
4. You suspect an unauthorized account was created.

## [INTENT] "Show me every user and what they can do."

## [CONTEXT]
A security audit checks: Who has accounts? Who can use sudo?
Who logged in recently? What files are world-writable?

## [ACTION] Audit commands
  cat /etc/passwd | cut -d: -f1         — list all usernames
  cat /etc/group | grep sudo            — who is in sudo group?
  last -5                               — last 5 logins
  who                                   — currently logged in
  w                                     — logged in + activity
  find / -perm -002 -type f 2>/dev/null — world-writable files
  find / -perm -4000 2>/dev/null        — setuid binaries

## [EXPECTED RESULT]
A complete picture of who has access and what they can do.

## Try it now:
  who
  last -3
  cat /etc/passwd | cut -d: -f1 | sort
