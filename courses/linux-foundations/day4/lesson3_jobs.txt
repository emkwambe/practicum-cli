# Lesson: jobs, bg, fg — Background and Foreground Control

## [WHEN] You need this when:
1. You started a long-running command and want your terminal back.
2. You accidentally started something in the foreground and need to background it.
3. You want to see what background tasks are running in your shell.
4. You need to bring a background task back to interact with it.

## [INTENT] "Run this in the background" or "Show me my running jobs."

## [CONTEXT]
By default, commands run in the "foreground" — they block your terminal.
Adding & at the end runs them in the background immediately.
Ctrl+Z suspends (pauses) a foreground job — it does not kill it.
bg resumes a suspended job in the background.
fg brings a background job to the foreground.
jobs lists all jobs in the current shell session.

## [ACTION] command & / jobs / fg / bg
Common patterns:
  sleep 300 &                    — start in background
  jobs                           — list all jobs with status
  fg %1                          — bring job 1 to foreground
  bg %1                          — resume job 1 in background
  Ctrl+Z                         — suspend current foreground job

Job states:
  Running    — executing in background
  Stopped    — suspended with Ctrl+Z
  Done       — finished

## [EXPECTED RESULT]
Background jobs run without blocking your prompt.
jobs shows a numbered list with status.
fg/bg change where the job runs.

## Try it now:
  sleep 100 &
  sleep 200 &
  jobs
  fg %2
  (then Ctrl+Z)
  bg %2
  jobs
